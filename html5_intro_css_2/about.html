Behavior of a crow

Crows are verry social birds. Crows are known to flock together, and sometimes they can be seen flocking in 1000s. crows are extremely smart. crows are also known to be excellent learners and problem solvers; however, this has given them the reputation of being mischievous. They can also be aggressive when provoked. Often times they will fight other birds that are known to be larger and stronger such as hawks and owls.

Crow Facts

Here are some more interesting facts about crows. Crow siblings actually help the crow parents raise new chicks. Also, when a crow dies the crows who knew that bird will have a funeral of sorts. Scientists refer to crows as the ‘fathered apes’ infract, crow brain to body percentage is 0.26 ounces and accounts for 2.7 percent of its overall weight. This is actually a greater percentage than humans, with humans having a 3-pound brain which is 1.9 percent of overall weight.
